How it works

Attune plays carefully tuned tones so your ears — and, with headphones, your brain — hear a slow pulse at a chosen frequency. That pulse is the “beat.”

Two tones, one perceived beat

In binaural mode, each ear gets a slightly different pure tone — for example 200 Hz on the left and 210 Hz on the right. You never hear a 10 Hz sound from the speakers; instead, brainstem circuits that compare the two ears produce a 10 Hz difference signal. That only works with stereo headphones (or earbuds), because each ear must receive its own channel.

Isochronic mode is different: a single tone is turned on and off (or pulsed in volume) at the target rate. The beat is in the sound itself, so it works over speakers. Ambient mode keeps the binaural pair and adds a soft noise bed so the tones feel less stark.

Use headphones for binaural and ambient. Isochronic is fine on speakers.

Brainwave bands (rough map)

EEG research groups cortical rhythms into bands. Attune’s presets sit in the ranges people often associate with calm focus or alert thinking — not as medical claims, but as a practical map for choosing a tone.

~1–4 HzDelta — deep sleep (not offered here)
~4–8 HzTheta — drowsiness, light meditation
~8–12 HzAlpha — relaxed wakefulness, easy focus
~13–30 HzBeta — active thinking, problem-solving
~30–100 HzGamma — binding, high attention (debated)

What studies actually show

The idea that listening to a beat “entrains” your brain to that frequency is popular, but the evidence is mixed and often modest.

  • EEG work has sometimes found that binaural beats can modulate oscillatory power or connectivity, especially in alpha/beta ranges — but results vary by protocol, carrier frequency, and listener.
  • Behavioral studies on attention, anxiety, and memory report small or inconsistent effects. Meta-analyses tend to urge caution: many trials are small, and placebo or expectation effects are hard to rule out.
  • Isochronic tones and rhythmic stimulation more generally can affect subjective arousal and attention; again, effects are not guaranteed for every person.
  • 40 Hz stimulation has drawn interest in Alzheimer’s research (often using light and sound together in lab settings). Consumer audio alone is not a treatment.

A fair takeaway: these tones can be a pleasant, structured backdrop for focus or wind-down. Treat any “brain state” language as a guide for choosing a feel, not as clinical advice.

Why Attune softens the sound

Raw sine waves can feel harsh. Attune fades tones in and out, gently low-passes the carriers, and (in ambient mode) layers quiet noise so the beat sits in a softer texture. Volume defaults low — start quiet and raise only as needed.
